PROBLEM DEFINATION


The definition of the problem:
 In primitive areas across the globe, the difficulty of obtaining a reliable water supply poses a significant challenge. Limited infrastructure, geographical obstacles, technological deficiencies, resource constraints, and environmental factors contribute to this problem. The consequences are far-reaching, encompassing health risks, economic setbacks, educational hindrances, gender disparities, and social instability. To address these issues, the goal is to develop sustainable and scalable solutions that enhance water accessibility in primitive areas. This entails improving infrastructure to facilitate water supply systems, overcoming geographical challenges through innovative approaches, leveraging appropriate technologies for water collection and purification, allocating sufficient resources and funding, and considering the impact of environmental factors on water availability. By addressing these key factors, the aim is to improve the overall health, socio-economic conditions, and well-being of the communities affected by water scarcity. Ultimately, the goal is to create a reliable and sustainable water supply system that empowers primitive areas to thrive and overcome the challenges posed by limited access to water resources.
